Introduction

The dyanamism of infectious disease spread is often formulated with mathematical models. A common way to model epidemic spread is to consider that individuals progress though different epidemiological states over time. A simple example is the SIR model, in which individuals are classified as either susceptible (S) to infection, infected and infectious (I), or removed, recovered, immune or dead (R). The SIR is governed by three ODEs, \[ \frac{dS}{dt} &= - \frac{\beta I S} {N} \] \[ \frac{dI}{dt} &= \frac{\beta I S} {N} - \gammaI \] \[\frac{dS}{dt} &= \frac{\beta I S} {N}\] \[\int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} e^{-x^2}~dx = \sqrt{\pi}.\]
